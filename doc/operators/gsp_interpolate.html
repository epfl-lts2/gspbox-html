GSP_INTERPOLATE - Interpolation of a graph signal

Usage

f_interpolated=gsp_interpolate(f_subsampled, G, keep_inds);
f_interpolated=gsp_interpolate(f_subsampled, G, keep_inds, param);

Input parameters

f_subsampled A signal on the subset of the vertices of G indexed by keep_inds
G Graph structure.
keep_inds The vertex indices of \(V_1\)

Output parameters

f_interpolated Interpolated graph signal on G.

Description

'gsp_interpolate(f_subsampled,G,keep_inds)' performs the interpolation:

\begin{equation*} f_{interpolated}=\Phi_{{\cal V}_1} \Phi_{{\cal V}_1,{\cal V}_1}^{-1} f_{{\cal V}_1} = \Phi_{{\cal V}_1} \left[\bar{{\cal L}}_{{\cal V}_1,{\cal V}_1}-\bar{{\cal L}}_{{\cal V}_1,{\cal V}_1^c}(\bar{{\cal L}}_{{\cal V}_1^c,{\cal V}_1^c})^{-1}\bar{{\cal L}}_{{\cal V}_1^c,{\cal V}_1}\right] f_{{\cal V}_1} \end{equation*}

Note that keep_inds are the vertex indices of \(V_1\); i.e., those where the signal values are available to use in the interpolation.

param contains the following fields

  • param.order : Degree of the Chebyshev approximation (default=100 to yield a good approximation of the default Green's kernel). This parameter is passed to gsp_filter_analysis.
  • param.regularize_epsilon : The regularized graph Laplacian is \(\bar{L}=L+\epsilon I\) (default = 0.005). A smaller epsilon may lead to better regularization, but will also require a higher order Chebyshev approximation.

References:

I. Pesenson. Variational splines and paley--wiener spaces on combinatorial graphs. Constructive Approximation, 29(1):1--21, 2009.
